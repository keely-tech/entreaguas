The destination

The coastline Brazil's most discerning travellers have kept to themselves.

Most Europeans haven't heard of São Miguel dos Milagres. Most Brazilians who matter have been going there for years. That gap is exactly where the value lives.

Milagres sits on the Costa dos Corais — the second largest coral reef system in the world — in the state of Alagoas. The water is warm, crystalline, and calm year-round. Natural tide pools form when the tide recedes. The Tatuamunha River is one of the few places in Brazil where you can still observe manatees in their natural habitat.

This is already where Brazil's ultra-wealthy spend New Year's Eve. Actress Glória Pires owns a compound here that rents for approximately R$8,000 per night. The destination is validated at the very highest level of Brazilian domestic demand. What it hasn't had is a structured access point for European buyers. That changes now.

How European buyers own this

You own it outright. Here is how.

◈

Full foreign ownership

Brazil permits direct property ownership by foreign nationals — no restrictions on non-residents. Protected under Brazilian civil law and constitutional property rights. A European buyer holds exactly the same ownership rights as a Brazilian buyer.

◈

Freehold equivalent

Brazilian property law uses plena propriedade — full ownership — recorded via escritura pública (public deed) at the local cartório. Absolute, transferable, and inheritable. The functional equivalent of freehold title in any European jurisdiction.

◈

Legally incorporated development

ENTREÁGUAS MILAGRES is a confirmed, legally registered development. Real Estate Incorporation Registry: R-1, registration no. 5,648, Porto de Pedras/AL. Not a pre-registration concept — the legal foundation is in place and independently verifiable.
